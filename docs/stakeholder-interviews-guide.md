# Stakeholder Interviews & Scope Approval Guide (1.2)

This guide outlines the process for conducting stakeholder interviews and obtaining scope approval for the Fellowus project.

## Objectives

1. Understand stakeholder expectations and requirements
2. Align on project scope and priorities
3. Identify potential risks and constraints
4. Obtain formal approval to proceed

## Stakeholder Identification

### Primary Stakeholders

- **Product Owner**: Defines product vision and priorities
- **Business Stakeholders**: Define business goals and KPIs
- **Engineering Lead**: Technical feasibility and constraints
- **Design Lead**: User experience and design requirements
- **Marketing**: Go-to-market strategy and positioning

### Secondary Stakeholders

- **Legal/Compliance**: Privacy, data protection, terms of service
- **Support**: User support and feedback channels
- **Operations**: Infrastructure and deployment requirements

## Interview Preparation

### Pre-Interview

1. **Review Existing Documentation**:
   - Product Brief (`docs/01-discovery/PRODUCT_BRIEF.md`)
   - PRD (`docs/01-discovery/PRD.md`)
   - Scope document (`docs/01-discovery/SCOPE.md`)
   - Success metrics (`docs/01-discovery/SUCCESS_METRICS.md`)

2. **Prepare Questions**:
   - Business goals and success criteria
   - Feature priorities and must-haves
   - Timeline expectations
   - Budget and resource constraints
   - Risk tolerance

3. **Schedule Interviews**:
   - 30-60 minutes per stakeholder
   - Allow time for follow-up questions
   - Record sessions (with permission)

## Interview Questions

### Product Owner

- What is the primary goal of Fellowus?
- Who is the target audience?
- What are the top 3 must-have features for v1.0?
- What features can wait until v2.0?
- What does success look like in 3 months? 6 months? 1 year?
- What are the biggest risks or concerns?

### Business Stakeholders

- What are the business KPIs we should track?
- What is the go-to-market strategy?
- What is the budget and timeline?
- What are the revenue expectations (if applicable)?
- What are the competitive differentiators?

### Engineering Lead

- What are the technical constraints?
- What is the preferred tech stack?
- What are the scalability requirements?
- What are the security and compliance requirements?
- What is the deployment strategy?

### Design Lead

- What is the target user experience?
- What are the design principles?
- What are the accessibility requirements?
- What are the localization requirements?
- What are the brand guidelines?

### Marketing

- What is the positioning strategy?
- What are the key messaging points?
- What are the launch channels?
- What are the content requirements?
- What are the analytics requirements?

## Interview Process

### During Interview

1. **Introduction** (5 min):
   - Explain purpose and agenda
   - Set expectations for follow-up

2. **Discovery** (20-40 min):
   - Ask prepared questions
   - Allow for open discussion
   - Capture key points

3. **Clarification** (5-10 min):
   - Summarize key points
   - Clarify any ambiguities
   - Identify action items

4. **Wrap-up** (5 min):
   - Thank participant
   - Confirm next steps
   - Schedule follow-up if needed

### Post-Interview

1. **Document Findings**:
   - Summarize key points
   - Identify themes and patterns
   - Document decisions and action items

2. **Share Summary**:
   - Send summary to participant for confirmation
   - Share with team for alignment

3. **Follow-up**:
   - Address any questions or concerns
   - Schedule additional interviews if needed

## Scope Approval Process

### 1. Compile Findings

- Consolidate interview findings
- Identify common themes
- Document priorities and constraints
- Highlight any conflicts or concerns

### 2. Create Scope Document

- Update `docs/01-discovery/SCOPE.md` with approved scope
- Document out-of-scope items
- Define success criteria
- Set timeline and milestones

### 3. Review with Team

- Present findings to team
- Discuss priorities and trade-offs
- Align on approach
- Address concerns

### 4. Obtain Approval

- Present scope to stakeholders
- Address questions and concerns
- Obtain formal sign-off
- Document approval in project status

## Approval Checklist

- [ ] All primary stakeholders interviewed
- [ ] Interview findings documented
- [ ] Scope document updated
- [ ] Priorities aligned
- [ ] Timeline agreed upon
- [ ] Budget approved (if applicable)
- [ ] Risks identified and mitigated
- [ ] Formal sign-off obtained
- [ ] PROJECT_STATUS.md updated

## Common Challenges

### Conflicting Priorities

**Solution**: Use prioritization framework (P0/P1/P2), involve Product Owner in decisions

### Unclear Requirements

**Solution**: Ask clarifying questions, document assumptions, validate with stakeholders

### Scope Creep

**Solution**: Maintain clear scope document, refer back to original goals, use change request process

### Timeline Pressure

**Solution**: Be realistic about timelines, identify must-haves vs nice-to-haves, communicate trade-offs

## Next Steps

After scope approval:

1. Update project roadmap
2. Create detailed task breakdown
3. Set up project tracking
4. Begin development
5. Schedule regular check-ins
